Cosmos Policy: Fine-Tuning Video Models for Visuomotor Control and Planning

AI generated summary.

arXiv: 2601.16163  ·  Venue: conference (ICLR-style final copy)
Authors: Moo Jin Kim, Yihuai Gao, Tsung-Yi Lin, Yen-Chen Lin, Yunhao Ge, Grace Lam, Percy Liang, Shuran Song, Ming-Yu Liu, Chelsea Finn, Jinwei Gu (NVIDIA + Stanford)
Project page: research.nvidia.com/labs/dir/cosmos-policy

One-line summary

Take a pretrained video diffusion model (Cosmos-Predict2-2B). With zero architectural changes and a single stage of fine-tuning, turn it into a robot policy that jointly predicts actions, future states, and values. Then use those predictions for test-time model-based planning (best-of-N).

The core idea: latent frame injection

The whole trick is how non-image modalities are fed to a video model without touching its architecture:

  1. Video is tokenized by a VAE into a sequence of latent frames of shape (1+T') × H' × W' × 16.
  2. New modalities (robot proprioception, action chunk, scalar value) and extra camera views are interleaved as additional latent frames in that sequence.
  3. Low-dim vectors (e.g. an action chunk K × d_act) are normalized to [-1, +1], flattened, and duplicated/tiled to fill an H' × W' × C' latent volume, overwriting a blank placeholder frame.
  4. Everything is trained with the same EDM denoising objective the base model already uses. No new heads, no separate action diffuser, no inverse dynamics model.
  5. At inference, extract a modality by averaging across its duplicated copies in the denoised latent and un-normalizing. Non-image modalities need no VAE decode.

The sequence ordering (s, a, s', V(s')) supports two decode modes:

  • Parallel decoding: fast, used for the direct policy.
  • Autoregressive left-to-right decoding: higher quality, used for planning.

Unified policy + world model + value function

One model, three roles, selected purely by the conditioning mask (which latent frames are clean vs. denoised):

  • Policy: p(a, s', V(s') | s), with auxiliary future-state and value targets, not just p(a|s).
  • World model: p(s', V(s') | s, a).
  • Value function: p(V(s') | s, a, s'), Monte Carlo returns, sparse terminal reward γ^{H-t} R(s_H, a_H).

Training batches are split (e.g. 50% demos to policy, 25% rollouts to world model, 25% rollouts to value). Masking the value inputs lets the same machinery represent either V(s') (needs world model) or Q(s,a) (model-free).

Planning

  • Train-on-demos-only is insufficient for planning. The world model and value only see successful, narrow state distributions.
  • Collect policy rollout data (successes and failures). Fine-tune a separate "planning model" with heavy weighting (90% world-model+value, 10% policy). Keep the original checkpoint as the "policy model". This dual deployment keeps the world model trained on on-policy data.
  • Best-of-N: sample N action chunks from the policy, imagine future state per chunk, score by predicted value, execute the highest-value chunk. Ensemble (3 world-model × 5 value = 15 value preds per action), aggregate via "majority mean" for robustness to bimodal predictions.
  • Parallelize N over N GPUs.

Two practical training tricks

  1. Noise schedule matters for actions. The base video model's log-normal σ sampling under-weights high noise levels. Actions need precision, so they use a hybrid log-normal + uniform (0.7/0.3) with a fatter high-σ tail. At test time they raise σ_min from 0.002 to 4, since the last near-zero denoising steps are low-SNR and hurt action accuracy.
  2. Auxiliary future-state supervision carries the method. RoboCasa ablation: removing world+value samples drops 67.1 to 64.0. Removing the future-state auxiliary target on the policy collapses it to 44.4%. Predicting the future is doing most of the work, not just the value.

Headline results

  • LIBERO: 98.5% avg (SOTA), beating π₀.₅, OpenVLA-OFT, CogVLA, and others.
  • RoboCasa: 67.1% avg with only 50 demos/task (others use 300 to 3000).
  • Real ALOHA bimanual: 93.6% avg score. Planning adds +12.5 pts on the two hardest tasks.
  • Ablations: no pretrained weights drops 3.9 pts (video priors matter). Model-based V(s') planning beats model-free Q(s,a) under limited rollout data.
  • Cost: about 0.61s per action-chunk (5 steps) as a direct policy. Planning search is about 4.9s on 8 GPUs, too slow for dynamic tasks (their stated limitation).
